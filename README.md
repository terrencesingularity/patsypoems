# Patsy Poetry

This repository gathers the poems, sonnet sequences, fragments, and formal studies that belong to my ongoing Patsy project.
|
It's abouta  woman I should have known was in Brooklyn when I lived there for five years in the 90s.  But somehow, when I look back now, I did not know
she was there.  Right now March 31, 2026, this is the part I have been struggling with.  Even though I saw her at a party on Avenue D in Manhattan, and there
ensued a kind of exchange and subsequent drama--it ended with her turning away from me without speaking, twice.  And somehow, after that, it was as if the
very knowledge of her was withdrawn from my mind.  It was not like normal forgetting.

At the center of the work is a single figure, a single absent woman, and a single continuing pressure on memory: Patsy as presence, riddle, threshold, and governing form. These poems are attempts to hold that pressure in language without explaining it away.

The project moves through love poetry, memory poetry, city poetry, and sonnet study. Brooklyn appears here. So do scenes of encounter, aftermath, misrecognition, longing, silence, and return. Again and again the poems circle the same problem: how to speak precisely about an event that feels both intimate and world-altering.

## What this repository is

This is not a finished book in static form. It is a working poetry archive.

It includes:

- sonnets and sonnet sequences
- formal experiments
- governing-form studies
- lyric fragments and draft pages
- notes on structure, tone, and recurring motifs

Some pieces are polished. Some are still being discovered.

## Why the sonnet

The sonnet is important to this project because it is a heat- & pressure-form. It can hold argument, desire, breakage, memory, reversal, and aftermath in a very small space. 

That makes it the right instrument for this material.

Different sonnet forms may appear here when the material demands them. The aim is not academic display, but exact fit: the right vessel for the turn of feeling, thought, or revelation.

## What the work is trying to do

This poetry is trying to make a coherent field out of scattered moments.

It is trying to build continuity where there was interruption, form where there was shock, and music where there was silence.

It is also trying to see whether a long poetic sequence can do something a memoir or explanation cannot: preserve the mystery of what happened while still giving it shape.

## Reading note

This repository is best read as an unfolding sequence rather than a set of isolated poems. Images, phrases, names, and situations recur. Meanings deepen by repetition.

You do not have to begin at the beginning, but if you stay with the sequence, patterns will begin to emerge.
